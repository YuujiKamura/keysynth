License regimes used by the entries selected for this corpus
(restricted to the permissive subset):

  1. Public Domain — works whose copyright has expired worldwide.
     Catalogued on Mutopia as "Public Domain". Treated equivalently
     to CC0 for redistribution purposes.

  2. Creative Commons CC0 1.0 Universal — explicit dedication of
     a work (typesetting + arrangement) to the public domain.
     URL: https://creativecommons.org/publicdomain/zero/1.0/

  3. Creative Commons Attribution (CC-BY) 3.0 / 4.0 — attribution
     required, redistribution permitted including commercial use.
     URL: https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/3.0/
     URL: https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/

  4. Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike (CC-BY-SA) 3.0 / 4.0 —
     attribution and ShareAlike required.
     URL: https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/3.0/
     URL: https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/4.0/

EXCLUDED from this corpus on purpose:
  - Any Mutopia entry tagged Creative Commons NonCommercial (CC-BY-NC*).
  - Any entry tagged "Permissions: not for redistribution" or carrying
    typesetter-imposed restrictions beyond the four regimes above.

Per-piece attribution + license type is recorded in
bench-out/REF/guitar/corpus/classical/manifest.json under the
`license`, `attribution`, and `mutopia_id` fields. The Mutopia
catalogue page URL for each piece is the canonical provenance link
and is also stored per-entry.

Dataset summary:
  - Public-domain classical / Renaissance / Baroque guitar literature
  - LilyPond source + auto-rendered MIDI per piece (we use the MIDI
    only — LilyPond → MIDI is part of Mutopia's automated build)
  - Total catalogue spans hundreds of pieces; this corpus selects ~10
    canonical works with definite public-domain composer status
    (Bach, Sor, Tárrega, Carcassi, Giuliani, Carulli) typeset by
    contributors who released their typesetting under Public Domain
    or CC0.

Use in this corpus:
  Only the auto-rendered .mid files are reproduced (downloaded on
  demand by fetch.sh). For each piece we also fetch a synthesised
  reference WAV produced by Mutopia's own LilyPond → MIDI → fluidsynth
  pipeline (where available); failing that, fetch.sh leaves the MIDI
  in place and the WAV slot empty (the manifest.json marks
  `audio_available: false`).

  LilyPond .ly source files are NOT mirrored — they sit at the
  upstream URL listed in manifest.json `source_url`.
